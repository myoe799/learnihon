Frequently Asked Questions

Want to live & study in Japan?

We will do our best to support you during your application process, all the way until your arrival in Japan! Here are some of the frequently asked questions that we get:

Q: I want to enrol in a long term course. Why do I need to apply months in advance before I begin my study?

A: You need to apply for a student visa to enrol in a long term course, and the procedure for student visa application could take several months. Hence, it is advised that you prepare for your application well in advance.

Q: Can you tell me about this procedure?

A: Simply put: students need to submit all the required documents and pay the application fee to the school. The school will then forward the documents to Japanese immigration bureau in Japan in order to issue student's Certificate of Eligibility (CoE). Once CoE is issued (around 2-3 months), the original CoE will be sent to the student. Student can then apply for a student visa with Japanese embassies in their respective country using the original CoE.

Q: When does the school term start?

A: Generally, school term starts every January, April, July or October. There are also schools that accept students every month. Contact us for more details!

Q: How long do I need to study to become fluent in Japanese?

A: It varies for different people, but in general it takes about 2 years of study (for complete beginner). However, it might be possible to reach fluency faster if you work hard!

Q: I speak ZERO Japanese. Will I survive in Japan?

A: Absolutely. You will have a couple of months to learn some Japanese survival phrases before your departure to Japan, so we believe that you will have plenty of time to prepare. We also have free study materials for complete beginners, and we will try our best to answer any questions you have! Furthermore, the support staffs in each schools speak English so you will be fine even without any Japanese language ability.

Q: Is there any scholarship program offered by the schools?

A: It varies by school. Generally, scholarships are awarded to excellent students after enrolment. You can contact us for more details!

Q: Can I do any part time jobs in Japan?

A: If you have a student visa, you can get a permit to work part time for up to 28 hours per week. This should generally be enough to cover your living expenses in Japan.

Q: How much is the monthly living cost in Japan?

A: Varies by city. For a big city like Tokyo, it ranges between ¥70,000 up to ¥150,000 depending on students' lifestyle. For smaller cities, it can go as low as two thirds the living cost in Tokyo. You can contact us for a more detailed breakdown on living cost!

Q: Can I work in Japan after I finish my study in a Japanese language school?

A: You might need at least JLPT N2 ability if you wish to work with a Japanese company. You should be able to apply for jobs in Japan, provided that you have the qualifications, and you can apply for a work visa after you have found a job.

Q: Can you help me with document translations?

A: Absolutely. We will help you translate all your documents for FREE.

Q: What about finding an accommodation?

A: We will do our best to help you find suitable accommodation, according to your preference and budget.

Q: And airport pickup?

A: Yes, we can help you arrange an airport pickup, but there will be transportation charges. Alternatively, we can give you detailed directions on what to do and where to go from the airport so you won't get lost!

Q: You mentioned FREE services. Really?

A: Yes! We will not bill you anything! However, please understand that we cannot guarantee the outcome of your CoE/visa issuance since it is 100% up to Japanese immigration.

Q: Do we have to pay anything other that the fees listed on each schools' page?

A: You might have to pay for bank transfer fee when you pay the school fee and courier fee when you send your documents to the school, but we might have some exciting offers from time to time! Contact us for more details and check out our social media regularly for any updates!

Do you have any more questions that are not listed above?

Ask us now! Contact us for more information!
